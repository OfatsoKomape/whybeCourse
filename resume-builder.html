Build a Winning Resume

Craft a professional resume that stands out to employers in South Africa

Essential Resume Tips

  • Keep it concise: 1-2 pages maximum for most professions. Recruiters typically spend only 6-7 seconds scanning a resume.
  • Tailor your resume: Customize for each job application by aligning your skills and experience with the job requirements.
  • Highlight achievements: Use action verbs (e.g., "managed," "developed," "increased") and quantify results where possible (e.g., "Increased sales by 30%").
  • Professional format: Use a clean layout with consistent formatting (fonts, spacing, bullet points). Avoid graphics unless in a creative field.
  • Include keywords: Mirror terms from the job description as many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to screen resumes.
  • South African specifics: Include ID number, driver's license if relevant, and state "References available on request" rather than listing them.
  • Proofread thoroughly: Have someone else review your resume for spelling and grammatical errors. Even small mistakes can eliminate you from consideration.
  • Use a professional email: Avoid nicknames or unprofessional email addresses (e.g., use name.surname@gmail.com rather than partyanimal92@gmail.com).

Resume Sections Guide

1. Contact Information

Full name (larger font), phone number with country code (+27), professional email, LinkedIn profile (if available), and physical address (optional in South Africa).

2. Professional Summary

3-4 sentences highlighting your years of experience, key skills, industry expertise, and career goals. Tailor this to each job application.

3. Work Experience

List in reverse chronological order with company name, dates of employment (month/year), job title, and 3-5 bullet points per position focusing on achievements rather than duties.

Work Experience Tips

  • Focus on recent and relevant experience (last 10-15 years unless earlier experience is particularly relevant)
  • Use the PAR (Problem-Action-Result) method to describe accomplishments
  • Include industry-specific metrics where possible (e.g., "Managed R2.5 million budget")
  • For career changers, emphasize transferable skills

4. Education

Institution name, qualification earned, year completed (include expected graduation date for current studies). List highest degree first. Include relevant certifications.

5. Skills

Technical skills (software, tools, languages) and soft skills (communication, leadership). Categorize if helpful (e.g., "Technical Skills," "Language Proficiency").

Skills Section Tips

  • Include both hard and soft skills relevant to the position
  • List specific software/programs you're proficient in (e.g., "Advanced Excel," "SAP ERP")
  • For language skills, indicate proficiency level (e.g., "Zulu (fluent)", "French (intermediate)")
  • Prioritize skills mentioned in the job description

Advanced Resume Strategies

ATS Optimization

  • Use standard section headings (e.g., "Work Experience" rather than "Where I've Worked")
  • Incorporate keywords from the job description naturally throughout your resume
  • Avoid tables, columns, headers/footers, and graphics as these can confuse ATS systems
  • Save as a Word document (.docx) unless specified otherwise (PDFs are generally ATS-friendly but some older systems may have issues)

Career Change Strategies

  • Lead with a strong summary that explains your transition
  • Create a "Relevant Experience" section if much of your background isn't directly related
  • Highlight transferable skills that apply to the new field
  • Consider a functional or hybrid resume format if your work history isn't linear

For Recent Graduates

  • Place education before work experience if your degree is your strongest qualification
  • Include relevant coursework, projects, and academic achievements
  • Highlight internships, volunteer work, and extracurricular activities that demonstrate relevant skills
  • Include a "Projects" section if you have limited work experience

Industry-Specific Advice

  • IT/Tech: Include specific programming languages, frameworks, and technical projects
  • Finance: Highlight financial modeling, analysis, and relevant certifications
  • Healthcare: List licenses, certifications, and specialized training
  • Creative Fields: Include a link to your portfolio but keep the resume text-based
