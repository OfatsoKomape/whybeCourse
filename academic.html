Your Academic Pathway to Success

Understand the academic requirements for your chosen career path. Find information about courses, qualifications, and bursary opportunities at South African institutions.

Career Pathways

Engineering Career Pathway

  • High School: Focus on Mathematics (80%+), Physical Science (75%+), and English. Take additional technical subjects if available.
  • Undergraduate: 4-year Bachelor of Engineering (BEng) or Bachelor of Science in Engineering (BSc Eng). Specializations include Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, Chemical, etc.
  • Certification: Register with Engineering Council of South Africa (ECSA) after graduation.
  • Postgraduate (Optional): Master's degree for specialization or management roles.
  • Professional: Gain practical experience through internships and in-service training.

Health Sciences Pathway

  • High School: Focus on Life Sciences (75%+), Physical Science (70%+), Mathematics (60%+), and English.
  • Undergraduate: 5-6 year MBChB for Medicine, 4-year BSc for other health fields (Nursing, Physiotherapy, etc.)
  • Internship: 1-2 years of compulsory community service for doctors.
  • Specialization: Additional 4-6 years for medical specialists after internship.
  • Registration: Register with HPCSA (Health Professions Council of South Africa).

Business/Finance Pathway

  • High School: Mathematics (60%+), English, Business Studies, Accounting recommended.
  • Undergraduate: 3-year Bachelor of Commerce (BCom) with specializations like Accounting, Finance, Management, etc.
  • Postgraduate: 1-year Honours degree required for many professional paths.
  • Certification: Additional qualifications like CA(SA) (5-7 years total), CFA, or MBA for advancement.
  • Practical: Articles/training contracts required for accounting qualifications.

Information Technology Pathway

  • High School: Mathematics (60%+), IT/Computer Applications Technology recommended.
  • Undergraduate: 3-year Bachelor of Science in Computer Science or IT. Alternatives include diplomas and certificates.
  • Certifications: Vendor certifications (Microsoft, Cisco, AWS) valuable alongside degree.
  • Specialization: Fields like Cybersecurity, Data Science, Software Development may require postgraduate study.
  • Portfolio: Practical projects and GitHub repository important for employment.

Arts/Humanities Pathway

  • High School: Strong English marks important. History, Geography, Arts subjects beneficial.
  • Undergraduate: 3-year BA degree with majors in chosen fields (Psychology, Sociology, Languages, etc.)
  • Postgraduate: Often required for professional careers (e.g., Honours for Psychology route).
  • Practical Experience: Internships and volunteer work crucial for many arts/humanities careers.
  • Alternative Paths: Many careers in this field value experience and portfolio over formal qualifications.

Understanding APS Scores

The Admission Point Score (APS) is used by South African universities to evaluate applicants. Points are allocated for each matric subject based on your final percentage:

  • 80-100% = 7 points
  • 70-79% = 6 points
  • 60-69% = 5 points
  • 50-59% = 4 points
  • 40-49% = 3 points
  • 30-39% = 2 points
  • 0-29% = 1 point

Most universities calculate APS using your best 6 subjects (excluding Life Orientation). Always check with individual institutions as requirements vary.

Bursary Guide

Financial aid opportunities for South African students:

  • NSFAS (National Student Financial Aid Scheme)
    Full funding for tuition, accommodation, and living expenses for households earning < R350k/year. Covers most public universities and TVET colleges.
  • Funza Lushaka Bursary
    Full-cost bursary for students pursuing teaching qualifications. Recipients must teach at public schools for same number of years they received funding.
  • Department of Health Bursaries
    For medical, dentistry, pharmacy, and other health-related studies. Service obligation after graduation.
  • Thuthuka Bursary Fund
    For African and Colored students studying Chartered Accounting. Includes academic support and mentorship.
  • Transnet Bursary
    For engineering, transport economics, and related fields. Covers tuition, accommodation, and book allowance.
  • Sasol Bursary
    For engineering, science, and technology studies. Includes vacation work and potential employment after graduation.
  • Nedbank External Bursary
    For students in financial need studying commerce, science, or engineering. Academic merit considered.
  • Merit Bursaries
    Offered by most universities for high-achieving students (typically 80%+ average). Amounts vary by institution.

Bursary Application Tips

  • Start researching bursaries in Grade 11 as many have early deadlines
  • Prepare certified copies of ID, matric results, and proof of income
  • Write a compelling motivation letter explaining your circumstances and career goals
  • Apply to multiple bursaries to increase your chances
  • Meet all deadlines - late applications are rarely considered
  • Follow up on your application status if you don't hear back

Choosing the Right University

  • Program Strength: Some universities specialize in certain fields (e.g., Wits for health sciences, Stellenbosch for agriculture)
  • Location: Consider living costs and distance from home
  • Campus Culture: Visit open days to get a feel for the environment
  • Graduate Employment: Research which universities have strong industry connections in your field
  • Accommodation: Check availability and costs of residences
  • Language Policy: Some universities teach primarily in Afrikaans or English

TVET vs University

TVET Advantages:

  • More hands-on, practical training
  • Shorter duration (1-3 years)
  • Lower entry requirements
  • Often more affordable
  • Strong industry links for employment

University Advantages:

  • Broader theoretical foundation
  • Required for certain professions (doctors, lawyers, engineers)
  • Greater research opportunities
  • More diverse social/academic environment
